The Path

The path toward super intelligence will probably be a way shorter than the one from
narrow to general intelligence.

One of the first steps necessary to advance in AI technology is improve computational power.
Luckily, the computational power of hardware is accelerating at a steady rate.
It's so steady that it's even raised a theory known as Moore's Law. The law says that computational power doubles every two years. This law has been accurate so far!

The next step is improving software. One of the main ways of doing that is by mimicking the brain through neutral networks.

Neutral Networks

Neurons active Input to Output. Neural networks can be complicated, but there is a simplified version: They split information into a lot of smaller pieces.

When we get input in the first column, we then compare it to the neuron in the next column and the next column after that.
We do this until we get to the output layer and fint the best match.
This system is similar to that of the human brain.
After we provide the hardware and advance software systems, AI will have to carry on the work by itself through evolution .
That is where genetic algorithms come to play.

Genetic algorithms

Genetic algorithms work via survival of the fittest. A set of algorithms are created to solve a problem.
The most succesful ones are carried over to the next generation and crossed over to creat better versions of the algorithm. Eventually one of them is succesful in not only solving the problem but doing so in an optimal way. That will be the fittest algorithm.

It's through technics like these that AGI will eventually develop into ASI.
